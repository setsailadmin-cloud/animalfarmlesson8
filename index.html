Chapter 8 Core focus

In Chapter 8, the Fifth and Sixth Commandments are rewritten to justify alcohol consumption ("to excess") and executions ("without cause").

Napoleon negotiates a major timber sale, playing human neighbors Frederick and Pilkington against each other. He agrees to sell to Frederick for cash, which turns out to be counterfeit. Instantly, Frederick launches a brutal attack, blowing up the rebuilt Windmill in the Battle of the Windmill.

Key Historical Allegory: The timber trade and Frederick's violent invasion represents the **Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact (1939)** and Hitler's sudden, treacherous betrayal of the USSR via **Operation Barbarossa (1941)**.
